Hi, I’m Medha.
And this is my deep, dark forest — where 2+2 sometimes equals 5.  
Because that’s how my mind works: nonlinear, curious, and a little chaotic.

I keep running into interesting things — a curious data point, a strange historical twist, a market move I can't explain — and once I do, my mind can’t sit still. I want to know more. <br/>
And that’s when the spiral begins. <br/>
And then Loads of questions come knocking:

- Why are cocoa prices so jumpy?

- How do interest rates make world currencies dance?

- Why did Bacardi — yes, the rum company — bomb a Cuban oil refinery?

Sometimes I get pulled in deep. Other times, I just scratch the surface before another question hijacks my focus. It's not exactly linear, and it’s rarely “complete.” But this is how I think — by chasing what grabs my interest and seeing where it leads.

I learn best when I explain things in my own words. Big fan of the Feynman technique. If I can't write it clearly, I probably don't understand it yet. These notes are me trying to understand.

For years, my notes have been scattered — scribbled on loose sheets, old half-used notebooks, random Google Docs. But when I wanted to revisit a note months later, I often couldn’t find what I’d written, even though I knew I’d written _something_, _somewhere_. And because of this, I’ve lost lots of valuable notes. 

So this is me trying to be a little more organized.  
From now on, everything I’m exploring, questioning, and figuring out will live here — raw, messy, sometimes unfinished, but all in one place.

So now I’m keeping everything here.

This is my one-stop place for questions, rabbit holes, and messy thinking-in-progress. Not everything will be polished. Some topics may start and go quiet for a while — that usually means I followed a tangent that led me down another rabbit hole (don’t worry, I’ll be back eventually).

If you find any value, spark, or curiosity here, you’re more than welcome to hang out.
